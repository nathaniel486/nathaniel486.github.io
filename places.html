Historical Places to Visit

The Amstel House



The Amstel house is one of New Castle's oldest buildings dating back to the 1730's when Dr. John Finney, the wealthiest property owner in the area at the time built it. "the house is graced with original woodwork, fine architectural details and open hearth. Its early fanlight and central hallway were among the first uses of these features in the town. "
Source: http://www.newcastlehistory.org/houses/amstel.html



Immanuel Church



"Founded in 1689, Immanuel Church in New Castle, Delaware is one of the oldest Episcopal parishes in the United States. Immanuel's parishoners have been worshipping on the spot where the church stands for over 325 years. Our history and that of the town of New Castle have always been closely linked, and continue to be so today."
Source:http://www.immanuelonthegreen.org/



Battery Park



Battery park is a public park in the city bordered by West 3rd St, Delaware St, and the Delaware River. Each year the serpeation Day celebrations area held in the park with food, amsument park rides, a concert, and fire works over the Delaware River. Each Summer the City of New Castle hosts a series of free concerts for the public in the park.

The Cobblestone Road



The cobblestone road, located on Market st in the City of New Castle between the Courthouse and the town hall is the only original stone road in the state. Driving down this road can be fun, but is also really bumpy. During Driver's Ed class they made us drive down this road, and it wasn't exactly a pleasant experience for novice drivers. My parents wanted to drive down this road after their wedding in a horse drawn carriage, but were disappointed to discover the Art on the Green festival was taking place that day and had the road closed.



George Read II's House



George Read II’s Home located at 42 The Strand was built next to his Father’s house where he resided most of his life including when he served in the Continental Congress, was constructed between 1798 and 1803 and is around 14,000 Sq. ft and has a total of 22 rooms.

The Read house has been owned by two other families, but in 1975 the Lairds, the last family to own the house, donated the property along with it’s roughly 2 acres of gardens to the Delaware Historical Society where it is now a museum. Most of the rooms in the house have been renovated to what they probably would have looked like when the house was originally constructed. They even did chemical testing on the walls to match what the original colors of the walls were.
