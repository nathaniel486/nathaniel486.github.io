Festivals and Traditions


Separation Day

Source: http://rivista-cdn.delawaretoday.com/images/customdata/
2114_1.jpg?ver=1280432247

Seperation Day is a Delawarian holiday celebrted every year on the second saturday in June in rememberance of Delaware seperating from Pennsylvaian and Bristish authority on June 15, 1776 and declaring it's independance as a free state. Each year there is a parade down Delaware St starting at St. Peter's Church and ending at the New Castle Court House in New Castle, Delware. Afterward there is a festival in Battery park with food, amusement park rides, andconcerts, all concluding in fireworks on the Delaware River.

Art on the Green

Art on the Green is an Annual Art and Craft showcase hosted in Historic New Castle, Delawre. This annual event was started in 1970 and is hosted on the 4th Saturday in September each year on on The Green and in Battery Park.

A Day in Olde New Castle

Source: http://northdelawhere.happeningmag.com/wp-content/uploads/Old-New-Castle-Delaware-Festival-2013.gif

A Day in Olde New Castle is an annual Festival held in the Spring where visitors can come to the town and visit many of the historic buildings all through out the town. Many of the People participating dress up in colonial outifits. There are also events held in front of the Courthouse with people dancing and having a general good time.
